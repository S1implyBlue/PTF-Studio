Image format and palette limits

  • Use transparent PNG artwork while designing. PTF Studio converts supported assets to PSP-compatible indexed GIM data during export.
  • Icon artwork is limited to an indexed palette of no more than 256 RGBA entries. Complex gradients can band or dither after conversion.
  • Keep fully transparent pixels transparent and avoid hidden coloured pixels outside the visible artwork.
  • Use Import only for artwork already matching the exact canvas size. Use Downscale & Import for larger artwork.
  • Downscaling preserves the complete canvas and transparent borders. It does not trim to visible pixels or enlarge undersized artwork.

PSP-safe focus artwork

  • Focus graphics should be an outer white halo stored mainly in the alpha channel, not a second coloured copy of the icon.
  • Keep the original icon silhouette centred and leave 8 px of transparent room around it.
  • Use restrained blur and opacity. Heavy glow, glass effects, and complex transparency consume palette entries and may break or degrade on hardware.
  • Generated focuses are validated for dimensions, palette count, transparency, GIM round-trip, and final theme size before being accepted.

Design recommendations

  • Design with bold silhouettes, clean edges, and limited colour ramps. Tiny details often disappear on the PSP LCD.
  • Preview artwork against both dark and bright wallpapers. White XMB labels need sufficient contrast.
  • Do not crop transparent margins from icon canvases; PSP alignment depends on the full canvas, not only visible pixels.
  • Test the exported PTF on real hardware. The live viewer closely recreates the XMB but is not firmware emulation.

Theme limits and common failures

  • Keep the final PTF below approximately 768 KB. Large focus sets and complex palettes can exceed the practical PSP limit.
  • Do not invent unsupported record IDs. Later or model-specific XMB items should use the Default/Fallback icon where the PTF format has no safe dedicated slot.
  • Never overwrite the only copy of a working theme. Export to a new file and verify it on the PSP first.
